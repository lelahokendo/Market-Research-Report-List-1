<p><p>Hands-free scanners are categorized based on their connectivity types. Bluetooth scanners allow wireless scanning over short distances, enhancing mobility. Keyboard wedge scanners connect to a computer's keyboard port, facilitating direct data input. Serial scanners use a serial port for data transfer, suitable for legacy systems. USB scanners provide easy plug-and-play functionality, widely adopted in modern setups. Wireless scanners offer flexibility by connecting over Wi-Fi networks, catering to environments where mobility and convenience are essential. Each type serves specific operational needs.</p></p>
